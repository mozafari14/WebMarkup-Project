Cooking With Other Asian Immigrants in Rotterdam

Every dish tells a story — of culture, family, and belonging.

Cooking Together, Growing Together

In Rotterdam, food is a language that everyone understands. Preparing a meal — it’s more than cooking; it’s sharing, laughing, and remembering home. The Asian community in Rotterdam has created beautiful ways to connect through food, helping both newcomers and locals feel like they belong.

Through regular meetups, potluck dinners, and cooking classes, people from different Asian backgrounds share their traditional dishes, stories, and experiences.

Building a Community Through Food

  • Cooking together helps immigrants feel at home and valued.
  • Communities share their rich food traditions with others.
  • Learning local recipes helps bridge cultures and friendships.
  • Food brings comfort, warmth, and shared memories.

Why Cooking Together Matters

Cooking together is more than just food — it creates connection. Many immigrants find comfort and friendship through shared cooking spaces and meals. Whether it’s dumpling-making workshops or curry nights, these events build a bridge between cultures and generations.

Cooking Together events also:

  • Support cultural understanding and diversity.
  • Reduce loneliness and encourage teamwork.
  • Help newcomers feel integrated in Rotterdam.

Join or Start Your Own Cooking Group

Want to join?

  1. Visit Wah Nam Hong or Amazing Oriental Supermarket — many groups post events there.
  2. Follow the Asian Food Lovers Rotterdam page for community meetups.
  3. Ask your neighbors — sometimes, a friendly hello can lead to a shared meal!

To start your own group, gather friends, choose a recipe, and invite others to cook along. All it takes is a kitchen, a few smiles, and stories to share.
