The Taste of Home, Together!

Discover how food brings people together — sharing stories, recipes, and traditions from Asia to Rotterdam.

Learning Traditional Recipes

Food is more than just a meal — it’s a memory. Many Asian immigrants in Rotterdam share their favorite family recipes as a way of keeping their culture alive while connecting with others. From spicy curries and homemade noodles to fresh dumplings and soups, each recipe tells a story of love and belonging.

  • Watch cooking workshops and learn local Asian recipes.
  • Discover traditional ingredients from nearby stores.
  • Try recipes from China, Japan, Korea, India, Vietnam, and more.
  • Join our monthly “Cooking Together” meetups.

Sharing Culture Through Food

Many Asian families in Rotterdam host food-sharing events, potluck dinners, and recipe exchanges to connect new and long-term residents.

These gatherings include participants from all backgrounds and cuisines — from Filipino adobo and Japanese ramen to Indian curry and Korean kimchi pancakes. Every dish represents a part of someone’s journey and identity.

  • Meet people from China, Vietnam, the Philippines, India, and Japan.
  • Exchange recipes and personal cooking stories.
  • Enjoy traditional dishes made with locally found ingredients.
  • Support inclusivity through community cooking.

Where to Start?

You can begin your journey by visiting these local Asian supermarkets and restaurants:

  1. Amazing Oriental Supermarket (Westblaak)
  2. Wah Nam Hong (Westewagenstraat)
  3. Kyatcha Rotterdam (Otto Reuchlinweg)

Buy fresh ingredients, cook at home, and share your meal with a neighbor. Because the taste of home always feels better when shared.
